Paneer Purji

Description

Paneer also referred to as Indian cottage cheese is an important source of proteins for vegetarians. Paneer Bhurji Recipe is basically spiced scrambled paneer. This recipe is a Punjabi style of making this popular and delicious North Indian dish, just the way it is made in my home.

In this quick and simple vegetarian recipe, crumbled homemade paneer is cooked with a mixture of onions, tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies and some spices into a savory, tangy and satisfying dish.

You can actually take it as the vegetarian version of the Indian style scrambled eggs which is popularly known as egg bhurji or anda bhurji in Hindi language. The flavorful Paneer Bhurji Recipe, as like other bhurji recipes, is quick to make and an ideal breakfast dish too.

Ingredients

  • Cottage Cheese
  • Chopped Onions and Tomatoes
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Spices
    • Turmeric Powder
    • Red Chilli Powder
    • Coriander Powder
    • Garam Masala Powder

Steps

  1. Crumble 200 to 250 grams paneer with your fingers and keep aside. I have used homemade paneer.
  2. Heat 3 tablespoons oil in a frying pan or kadai (wok) or a sauté pan or frying pan. You can even use ghee or butter, instead of oil.
  3. Add ½ teaspoon cumin seeds and let them crackle on low to medium-low heat.
  4. Then, add ½ cup finely chopped onions.
  5. Sauté the onions on medium-low heat till translucent and soft.
  6. Next, add 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste and 1 or 2 chopped green chilies (about ½ to 1 teaspoon, chopped).
  7. Mix very well and sauté for some seconds till the raw aroma of ginger-garlic goes away.
  8. Add 1 cup tightly packed finely chopped tomatoes.
  9. Mix the tomatoes and begin to sauté on medium-low heat.
  10. Sauté the tomatoes till completely soft. You can also add a pinch of salt, so that the tomatoes cook faster.
  11. Now add all the ground spice powders:
    • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder (ground turmeric)
    • ½ teaspoon red chilli powder (or ¼ to ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper)
    • ½ teaspoon coriander powder (ground coriander)
    • ¼ to ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
  12. Mix very well and sauté for 5 to 6 seconds on low to medium-low heat.
  13. Add the crumbled paneer and salt as per taste.
  14. Mix thoroughly and cook for 1 minute. Don’t cook the paneer for a long time, as it hardens and looses its softness.
  15. Turn off the heat and add 3 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves. Mix very well.
  16. Serve Paneer Bhurji hot with roti, chapati, phulka, paratha, pav (dinner rolls) or bread, with a side of thinly sliced onions and lemon wedges.
